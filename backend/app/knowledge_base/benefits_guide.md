# Employee Benefits Guide

## Health Insurance

### Medical Plans

We offer three medical plan options to fit your needs:

#### PPO Plan (Preferred Provider Organization)
- **Monthly Premium**: $180 (employee), $420 (family)
- **Annual Deductible**: $1,500 individual / $3,000 family
- **Out-of-Pocket Max**: $5,000 individual / $10,000 family
- Network flexibility: See any provider, lower costs in-network
- No referrals needed for specialists

#### HMO Plan (Health Maintenance Organization)
- **Monthly Premium**: $120 (employee), $320 (family)
- **Annual Deductible**: $500 individual / $1,000 family
- **Copays**: $25 primary care, $50 specialist
- Must choose primary care physician
- Referrals required for specialists

#### HDHP (High Deductible Health Plan) with HSA
- **Monthly Premium**: $80 (employee), $220 (family)
- **Annual Deductible**: $3,000 individual / $6,000 family
- **HSA Employer Contribution**: $1,000 individual / $2,000 family
- Pair with tax-advantaged Health Savings Account
- Best for healthy individuals who want to save on premiums

### Dental Insurance
- PPO coverage with nationwide network
- **Premium**: $25/month (employee), $65/month (family)
- Preventive care: 100% covered (cleanings, exams)
- Basic procedures: 80% covered after $50 deductible
- Major work: 50% covered after deductible
- Annual maximum benefit: $2,000 per person

### Vision Insurance
- **Premium**: $12/month (employee), $28/month (family)
- Annual eye exam: 100% covered
- Prescription glasses or contacts: $150 allowance every year
- Additional 20% discount on out-of-pocket costs

## Retirement Benefits

### 401(k) Plan

- **Employer Match**: 50% on first 6% of salary contributed
  - Example: If you contribute 6% ($6,000 on $100k salary), employer adds $3,000
- **Vesting**: Immediate 100% vesting on all contributions
- **Contribution Limits 2024**: $23,000 employee max, $30,500 if age 50+
- **Investment Options**: 25+ mutual funds, target-date funds, and company stock

### Roth 401(k) Option
- After-tax contributions with tax-free withdrawals in retirement
- Same employer match applies (match goes to traditional 401(k))

## Open Enrollment

### Annual Enrollment Period
- **Dates**: November 1-15 every year
- Coverage changes effective January 1
- Required action even if keeping current plans
- Default: Coverage drops if you don't enroll

### Qualifying Life Events (QLE)
Can make mid-year changes within 30 days of:
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption
- Loss of other coverage
- Change in employment status

Submit QLE form in Workday with supporting documentation.

## Additional Benefits

### Life Insurance
- **Company-Paid**: 1x annual salary (up to $500k max)
- **Voluntary**: Purchase additional coverage up to 5x salary
- **Dependent Coverage**: Available for spouse and children

### Disability Insurance
- **Short-Term Disability (STD)**: 60% salary replacement, up to 12 weeks
- **Long-Term Disability (LTD)**: 60% salary replacement after 90-day elimination period

### Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)
- **Healthcare FSA**: Up to $3,050/year for medical expenses
- **Dependent Care FSA**: Up to $5,000/year for childcare costs
- Use-it-or-lose-it: Spend by March 15 of following year

### Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Free confidential counseling (6 sessions/year)
- Financial planning and legal consultation
- Work-life resources
- 24/7 crisis support: 1-800-EAP-HELP

## Enrollment Instructions

### How to Enroll in Workday

1. Log in to Workday
2. Navigate to **Benefits** from home screen
3. Click **Benefits Enrollment**
4. Review current coverage
5. Click **Change Benefits** to modify
6. Select plans for each category
7. Add beneficiaries for life insurance
8. Review summary and submit
9. Confirmation email sent within 24 hours

### Need Help?

- **Benefits Team**: benefits@company.com or x5500
- **Open Enrollment Webinars**: Scheduled in October (calendar invites sent)
- **Benefits Comparison Tool**: Available in Workday under Resources
- **Insurance Carrier**: See ID card for customer service number
